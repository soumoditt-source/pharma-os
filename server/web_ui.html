👨‍⚕️ For Everyone

PharmaOS is like a virtual chemistry lab. You propose molecules (using SMILES — a text code), and the AI scores them like a real drug candidate:

  • Will it dissolve in your blood?
  • Will it cross into the brain?
  • Will it cause heart problems?
  • Is it easy to manufacture?

🧑‍🔬 For Chemists / Doctors

  • Lipinski Ro5 — MW <500, LogP <5, HBD≤5, HBA≤10
  • QED — Quantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (0→1)
  • SA Score — Synthetic Accessibility (1=easy, 10=hard)
  • TPSA — Polar Surface Area, drives oral/CNS permeability
  • hERG — Cardiac ion channel, high risk = cardiotoxicity
  • BBB — Blood-Brain Barrier penetration score
  • PyTorch ML — ESOL-trained MLP predicts solubility confidence

🚀 How to Play

  • 1. Click New Episode
  • 2. Search a drug (e.g. "Aspirin") → load its SMILES
  • 3. Modify the SMILES and click ▶ Step
  • 4. Watch your score improve with each iteration
  • 5. Aim for Score ≥ 1.0 (Lipinski) or ≥ 0.75 (QED)

⚗️ SMILES

Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System. A text notation for molecules.
CC(=O)Nc1ccc(cc1)O = Paracetamol / Acetaminophen

💊 Drug-likeness (QED)

Combines 8 molecular properties into one score (0–1). A score of >0.67 indicates a drug-like molecule. <0.25 is unlikely to be a drug.

🧲 Lipinski Rule of Five

  • MW < 500 Daltons
  • LogP < 5 (lipophilicity)
  • HBD ≤ 5 (H-bond donors: -OH, -NH)
  • HBA ≤ 10 (H-bond acceptors: O, N)

🫀 hERG Risk

hERG (K+ channel) blockade causes QT prolongation → dangerous arrhythmias. Score >0.6 = HIGH RISK. Most drug withdrawals are hERG-related.

🧠 Blood-Brain Barrier

BBB score >0.5 → CNS-active. Key for neurology/psychiatry drugs. Requires: TPSA <90Å², LogP 1–3, MW <400.

⚠️ PAINS

Pan Assay INterference Structures — molecules that give false positives in assays. These are flagged and penalized in scoring.

🤖 PyTorch ML Engine

A deep neural network trained on the DeepChem ESOL dataset using 2048-bit Morgan fingerprints. Predicts aqueous solubility (LogS) confidence in real-time.

🟢 Lipinski Optimizer (EASY)

Satisfy all 4 Lipinski Rule of Five criteria for oral drug bioavailability.

  • Max Steps: 10
  • Success: Score = 1.0 (all rules met)
  • Reward: +0.25 per rule satisfied
  • Penalty: −0.1 per violation above limit

🟡 QED Optimizer (MEDIUM)

Maximize QED score while avoiding PAINS structures.

  • Max Steps: 15
  • Success: QED ≥ 0.75
  • PAINS penalty: −0.20 per hit
  • Start: Molecule with QED ≤ 0.30

🔴 Multi-Parameter MPO (HARD)

Balance ALL properties simultaneously: QED + SA + Similarity + ADMET.

  • Max Steps: 20
  • Success: Composite ≥ 0.70
  • Formula: 0.35·QED + 0.25·SA + 0.20·Similarity + 0.20·ADMET
  • Most challenging — requires balancing 8+ properties

📡 RL Agent Scoring

Every step provides a reward signal 0.0 → 1.0. The final score is the maximum achieved. Usable for training RL agents via PPO/SAC.
